The portfolio team was pricing assets from six spreadsheets and a shared inbox. Every valuation was a small act of archaeology — and every delay cost a deal. ==This is the story of replacing archaeology with a signal.==

## Challenge

Data lived in personal drives; definitions of "value" differed by team; nobody trusted anyone else's number enough to act on it.

> We were drowning in spreadsheets before we could price a single asset.

## Solution

![The single pricing view, fed by sources teams already maintained.](placeholder)

We shipped a single pricing view fed by the sources teams already maintained — no new data entry, no new tool to learn. The signal updated nightly, and every team read the same number.

## Outcome

Time-to-price fell 63%. Four teams converged on one source of truth, and pricing stopped being a bottleneck in the deal cycle.
